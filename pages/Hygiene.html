PERSONAL HYGIENE

Personal hygiene is how you care for your body. This practice includes bathing, washing your hands, brushing your teeth, and more. Good personal hygiene habits are directly related to less illnesses and better health. They can also help you feel good about your appearance.

Every day, you come into contact with millions of outside germs and viruses. They can linger on your body, and in some cases, they may make you sick.

Poor personal hygiene habits, however, can lead to some minor side effects, like body odor and greasy skin. They can also lead to more troublesome or even serious issues. Poor hygiene habits can also affect your self-esteem. Looking and feeling presentable can give you a confidence boost and a sense of pride in your appearance.

HYGIENES
1. Bath Regularly

Wash your body and your hair often. Most people will benefit from a rinse at least every other day.

Showering with soap helps rinse away dead skin cells, bacteria, oils, and helps fight the spread of disease.

Shampooing your hair and scalp helps remove skin build up and protects against oily residues that can irritate your skin.

2. Trim your nails regularly to keep them short and clean

Tidying your nails helps you prevent spreading germs into your mouth and other body openings. You should also avoid biting your nails. Brush under them with a nail brush or washcloth to rinse away build up, dirt, and germs.

More Info

Keeping your finger and toenails trimmed and in good shape will prevent problems such as hang nails and infected nail beds.

3. Brushing Teeth

Brush at least twice a day for 2 minutes. Caring for your teeth and gums is a smart way to prevent gum diseases and cavities. Aim to brush after you wake up and before bed. If you can, brush after every meal, too. Floss between your teeth daily and you may use antibacterial mouthwash to help prevent tooth decay and eliminate pockets where bacteria and germs can build up.

More Info

Not brushing your teeth can lead to teeth issues and plaque build-up. Poor dental care is also a risk factor for several serious health issues.

4. Wash Your Hands

Germs on your hands can easily enter your body through your mouth, nose, eyes, or ears. Washing your hands is one of the most effective ways to reduce the spread of disease. Wash your hands:

More Info
  • when you handle food
  • when you handle garbage
  • any time you touch an animal
  • looking after anyone who is sick
  • treating a cut or wound
  • after going to the bathroom
  • after blowing the nose, coughing, or sneezing
  • After touching something dirty
  • Whenever hands look dirty

In order to achieve effective results, make sure you wash your hands properly. Scrub with soap for 20 to 30 seconds, and be sure to clean between your fingers, on the back of your hands, and under your nails. Rinse with warm water, and dry with a clean towel.

5. Wearing clean and tidy clothes daily

After you wear clothes, wash them with a detergent and dry them immediately upon rinsing. Dirty clothes can harbor bacteria that could lead to body odor or even skin irritations.

6. Keeping their rooms clean

Tidying your room also bring with itself some health benefits. When your bedroom becomes a peaceful and ordered place with no clutter around, you will feel less stressed and less distracted and easy to rest. Clean room will no more be home to bacteria and viruses that can compromise your health.

7. Cover your nose and mouth when coughing or sneezing

It stops germs from becoming airborne that spreads people around you. Throw used tissues in a bin and always wash hands with soap and water afterwards.

8. Eat Properly

Eat in right time and place. Choose healthy/nutritious food over junk food. Sitting up straight allows your food to digest uninterrupted.

9. Drink Plenty of Water

Water is much healthier than drinks that are high in sugar, sweeteners, additives and caffeine. Encourage yourself to drink plenty of water throughout the day, as even slight dehydration can lead to poor concentration, lethargy, irritability and headaches.

10. Sleep tight

Get plenty of rest. It is essential to your well-being. It is recommended to sleep 8 to 10 hours a night, so that you are refreshed and are ready to take on the day every morning.
